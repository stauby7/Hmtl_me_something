My Kidney Journey

Memorial Day of 2018 I found out that I recieved a diagnosis of ESRD or End Stage Renal Disease. I found out that my kidneys were running at about 4% operational capacity. It was very scary and I worried about my health and the future for my wife, Lacey, and my son, Carter. Roughly two days later, I had a surgery to put a port into my jugular artery. The idea behind port was to do hemodialysis to hopefully clean my blood to jumpstart my kidneys.

The treatment did not work so for a month and a half I had to travel to a center to have hemodialysis done on my body. About July I had a surgery to have a port put in my stomach to be able to do peneal dialysis, which is a less invacive way to clean the body of toxins then the dialysis I was on. After some healing and training on how to use the system, I was able to get the port in my neck removed and be able to be at home with the stomach port.

I resumed work like normal, well my new normal, like nothing was wrong. I had to do some general testing to be put on the transplant list and then I sat and went on with life for about a year and a half. I went through the day like normal until I got out of work and recived that fateful phone call.

My rep from Barnes Jewish Hospital called to say I may be a match for a donation but, only as back up, not primary. My wife and I got ready to go to St. Louis. We arrived late and stayed the night and after a few test. I was taken away to get my new organ. I went under and the next thing I knew it was late that night and I was in my hospital room with 21 staples. I spent about a week in the hospital then sent home and short of some every day medication, I'm healthy and happy.
